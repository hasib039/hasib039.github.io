FAQ · 008

Common questions answered.

What does a Business Analyst actually do in an Odoo project?

+

A Business Analyst is the bridge between your operations teams and the technical implementation. I conduct discovery workshops, document As-Is and To-Be processes using BPMN, write BRD/FRD/FSD specifications, manage UAT cycles, and ensure the system reflects your actual business — not a generic Odoo default. Without a BA, most ERP projects configure the wrong thing, correctly.

How long does an Odoo ERP implementation take in Bangladesh?

+

Timeline depends on scope: SME (3–5 modules, up to 25 users): 6–10 weeks. Mid-market (5–8 modules, 25–100 users): 3–6 months. Enterprise multi-site (100+ users, custom modules): 6–18 months. The biggest delays in any project are slow stakeholder availability during UAT and incomplete data for migration — not the software itself.

What is Metamorphosis Ltd. and why does it matter for my project?

+

Metamorphosis Ltd. is Bangladesh's first and leading Odoo Country Partner — officially certified by Odoo S.A. in Belgium. Working with a Country Partner means your implementation team is trained directly by Odoo's headquarters, you get priority access to new version upgrades, and there are formal support escalation paths to Odoo S.A. itself.

How much does Odoo ERP implementation cost in Bangladesh?

+

Typical ranges: SME deployment (3–5 modules, up to 25 users): BDT 2–5 lakh. Mid-market (5–8 modules, 25–100 users): BDT 6–15 lakh. Enterprise multi-site (100+ users, customization): BDT 15–40+ lakh. Use the free Cost Estimator above for a scoped estimate, or book a discovery call for a detailed quote.

Do you work on existing Odoo implementations or only greenfield projects?

+

Both. For existing implementations, I often conduct a health-check audit — reviewing the gap between what the system does and what the business needs, then defining a remediation plan. For greenfield projects, I start with a structured discovery phase. Either way, the process begins with understanding the business, not the software.
